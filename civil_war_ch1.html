Chapter 1 — The War Unnoticed

Parts 1–5

Part 1 — Malfunction

Guardian Training Bay 4 was supposed to be routine. Casey Vigor — The Isotope Hero — was running diagnostics on his isotope‑based energy output. But halfway through the session, his powers began to spike, distort, and collapse in unpredictable bursts.

Sparks of unstable isotope energy cracked across the room. Casey staggered, clutching his arm as his power core flickered violently.

Wave — The Power Copier — rushed in, shutting down the training grid. “Casey, that’s enough. You’re benched until we figure this out.”

Casey didn’t argue. He just lowered his head and walked out.

Part 2 — Alone at the Crash Site

Hours later, Casey found himself wandering the outskirts of the old Vexx Empire Crash Site. The twisted metal, scorched earth, and silent ruins reflected exactly how he felt — broken, unstable, and forgotten.

He sat on a collapsed Vexx transport wing, staring at his trembling hands. “What’s happening to me…?”

Part 3 — The Offer

A ripple of energy tore through the air. Three figures stepped out:

Martin Velaot — The 2nd Time Breaker God. David Kaine — The 2nd Arsonist. Viridian Viper — Nova Watch Commander.

“Casey Vigor,” Viridian said calmly. “We’ve been watching you. The Guardians don’t value you. They hide things. They lie. They use you.”

Martin added, “Nova Watch can give you purpose. Stability. A place where your power matters.”

Casey hesitated. Their words felt wrong — but they also felt comforting.

“I… I don’t know,” he whispered.

Part 4 — The Retrieval Team

Back at Guardian HQ, Seron sensed something was off. Nova Watch operatives near the crash site. Casey’s signal drifting. Something wasn’t right.

“Kole. Andrew. With me.”

Kole Rogue — The Original Arsonist — cracked his knuckles, flames flickering across his shoulders. Andrew OverDark — The 1st Time Breaker God — opened a temporal rift.

“We’re bringing Casey home,” Seron said.

Part 5 — The Argument

The two groups met in the ruins — Guardians on one side, Nova Watch on the other. Casey stood between them, confused, overwhelmed, and terrified.

Viridian Viper stepped forward. “Casey chooses his own path. You don’t own him.”

Kole snarled, “You’re manipulating him. Back off.”

Andrew’s voice echoed with temporal distortion. “This ends now.”

The tension snapped.

Seron arrived last, stepping through a dark rift. His voice was cold, sharp, and final:

“If Nova Watch wants a war… then I, Super Darkness, declare a Civil War.”
